Week 1: Los trabajos actuales y el presente perfecto

Focus: Introduce the unit theme, current youth jobs vocabulary, and review the present perfect tense to discuss work and volunteer experiences.

  • Vocabulary: Los trabajos actuales (cajero/a, camarero/a, consejero/a de campamento, niñero/a, salvavidas)
  • Grammar: El presente perfecto — review of form and irregular participles
  • Culture: Youth labor laws, why young people work, introduction to career collage
  • Modes: Interpretive Interpersonal Presentational
  • Weekly Gradebook Entry: Written reflection using the present perfect about work/volunteer experiences (Day 5)

1 Unit Launch & Career Interests CultureVocab Preview

▼
🎯 Daily Objective (Can-Do)
I can identify and discuss my personal interests and begin to connect them to possible careers. I can recognize vocabulary related to current youth jobs.
✅ Activities & Sequence (~45 min)
  1. Warm-Up — "¿Por qué trabajan los jóvenes?" (7 min): Display the question from Slide 4. Students do a 2-minute silent brainstorm on sticky notes (or Jamboard), then share at tables. Teacher collects 3–4 responses aloud. This activates prior knowledge and sets the unit theme.
  2. Can-Do Statements Introduction (5 min): Distribute or display the Can-Do Statements (from the pptx). Students self-assess where they are NOW on the progress bar. Explain they will revisit these at the end of the unit. Frame the unit: "By the end, you'll be able to talk about your future career in Spanish."
  3. Collage Activity — "¡Diseña tu collage!" (20 min): Adapted from Slide 3. Students create a digital collage (Google Slides, 1 slide) showing their interests and how these connect to future work. They add 3–5 images and label each in Spanish (even basic labels are fine). Play background music in Spanish.
  4. Gallery Walk + Partner Share (8 min): Students do a structured gallery walk viewing 4–5 classmates' collages. With a partner, they discuss: "¿Qué intereses tienes tú?" and "¿Qué trabajo puede conectar con este interés?" using a sentence frame on the board.
  5. Vocab Preview & Wrap-Up (5 min): Flash the 5 current jobs vocabulary (Slide 7: cajero, camarero, consejero de campamento, niñero, salvavidas) with images. Students repeat chorally. Quick informal poll: "¿Conoces a alguien con uno de estos trabajos?"
🔍 Check for Understanding
Exit Ticket: On a sticky note or Canvas text entry, students write 2 interests from their collage and 1 job from today's vocabulary that might connect. Sentence frame: "Me interesa ______ y un trabajo posible es ______."
🗣 Student Discourse Strategy
Structured Gallery Walk + Think-Pair-Share: Provide sentence frames on the board ("Yo creo que ______ puede ser un buen trabajo para ti porque…"). Assign partners rather than letting students choose — keeps 8th graders focused and on task. Set a visible timer for each rotation (2 min).
⚖ Differentiation
Heritage/Native Speakers ↑: Label collage items with full sentences explaining WHY that interest connects to a career. During gallery walk, they serve as "language models" — partner them strategically to help classmates form Spanish responses. Can add additional jobs beyond the 5 listed.
Additional Support ↓: Provide a pre-made word bank for collage labels (English + Spanish side by side). During gallery walk, allow bilingual sentence frames. The exit ticket can be completed with a word bank visible.

2 Los trabajos actuales — Deep Dive VocabularyCulture

▼
🎯 Daily Objective (Can-Do)
I can identify and describe 5 current youth jobs in Spanish and discuss youth labor laws in the U.S. and Spanish-speaking countries.
✅ Activities & Sequence (~45 min)
  1. Warm-Up — Vocab Recall (5 min): Display images of the 5 jobs (no labels). Students write the Spanish word for each in their notebooks. Quick check: volunteer answers, correct together.
  2. Vocab Deep Dive with Slides 7–8 (12 min): Present each job with context sentences from the unit presentation. For each job, ask: "¿Qué hace esta persona?" and "¿Dónde trabaja?" Students copy vocabulary into a graphic organizer (3 columns: Trabajo, ¿Qué hace?, ¿Dónde?).
  3. Youth Labor Laws Discussion (10 min): Use Slide 6. Teacher presents key facts about youth labor laws in Maryland (minimum age, minimum wage, hour restrictions). Then show a brief comparison with a Spanish-speaking country (e.g., Mexico or Spain). Students discuss at tables: "¿Son justas estas leyes? ¿Por qué sí o no?"
  4. Video: "Tipos de trabajos para jóvenes" (8 min): Play a short authentic video about youth employment (or use content from Slide 8). Students complete a simple viewing guide: list 3 jobs mentioned and 1 new detail they learned.
  5. Partner Interview — "¿Qué trabajo te interesa?" (10 min): Structured partner conversation. Student A asks 3 questions from a prompt card; Student B answers using the new vocabulary. Then switch. Questions: ¿Qué trabajo te interesa más? ¿Por qué? ¿Conoces a alguien que tenga este trabajo?
🔍 Check for Understanding
Quick Quiz on Canvas (5 matching items): Match the 5 jobs to their descriptions/images. Auto-graded, gives you immediate data on who needs re-teaching. Not a gradebook entry — formative only.
🗣 Student Discourse Strategy
Structured Partner Interview with Role Cards: Each student gets a card with their questions printed. This prevents off-task behavior because there's a clear script to follow. Circulate and listen — use a simple checklist to note who is using the target vocabulary. Set a timer: 4 min per partner, then switch.
⚖ Differentiation
Heritage/Native Speakers ↑: Add follow-up questions to the partner interview: "¿Cuáles son las ventajas y desventajas de este trabajo?" They can also brainstorm additional youth jobs not on the list and share with the class. During the labor laws discussion, they can share personal/family knowledge about work norms in their heritage country.
Additional Support ↓: Provide the graphic organizer partially filled in (2 of 5 jobs completed as models). For the partner interview, give response sentence frames: "Me interesa ser ______ porque ______." Video viewing guide includes word bank for possible answers.

3 El presente perfecto — Review & Reintroduction Grammar

▼
🎯 Daily Objective (Can-Do)
I can form the present perfect tense (haber + past participle) and use it to talk about recent work and life experiences.
✅ Activities & Sequence (~45 min)
  1. Warm-Up — "¿Qué has hecho hoy?" (5 min): Write the question on the board. Students brainstorm answers in pairs. Teacher asks 3–4 volunteers to share. This naturally reintroduces the present perfect without a formal grammar lecture first.
  2. Grammar Reintroduction with Slides 23–25 (12 min): Walk through the formation: haber (he, has, ha, hemos, han) + past participle (-ado, -ido). Highlight the irregular participles (hecho, escrito, dicho, visto, puesto, abierto, roto, vuelto, muerto, cubierto). Students copy the chart into notebooks. Emphasize: this was introduced in 3.1 — this is a review and deepening.
  3. Whiteboard Practice (8 min): Students use individual whiteboards (or notebook paper held up). Teacher gives infinitives; students write the present perfect form with a subject. "Yo / trabajar" → "Yo he trabajado." Do 8–10 rapid-fire examples, mixing regular and irregular. Immediate visual check.
  4. "Yo he… pero nunca he…" Speaking Chain (12 min): Students stand in a circle (or at desks in a chain). Each student says one thing they HAVE done and one thing they have NEVER done related to work/life: "Yo he trabajado como niñero pero nunca he trabajado como salvavidas." Next student must listen and add their own. This builds discourse AND present perfect reps.
  5. Written Practice (8 min): Worksheet or Canvas assignment: 8 sentences converting simple past to present perfect, all themed around work experiences. "Trabajé en un restaurante" → "He trabajado en un restaurante."
🔍 Check for Understanding
Whiteboard Spot-Check + Exit Ticket: During whiteboard practice, note which students consistently form the tense incorrectly (common errors: forgetting "haber," using wrong participle ending). Exit ticket: write 3 original present perfect sentences about your own experiences.
🗣 Student Discourse Strategy
"Yo he… pero nunca he…" Speaking Chain: This is a structured, low-stakes speaking activity where every student MUST participate. The chain format creates peer accountability. Post the sentence frame on the board. To manage 8th graders: keep the chain moving fast (max 15 seconds per student) and make it a game — if someone takes too long, their table group "helps."
⚖ Differentiation
Heritage/Native Speakers ↑: During the speaking chain, they must add a follow-up detail: "He trabajado como niñero y he aprendido a ser más paciente." On the written practice, they complete an extended version: write 5 original sentences using irregular participles specifically, then answer "¿Qué experiencia previa has tenido que te ayudaría en un trabajo futuro?"
Additional Support ↓: Provide a conjugation reference card for "haber" that stays on their desk. For the speaking chain, allow them to read from a pre-written sentence (they write it first, then read it aloud). Written practice includes the infinitive AND the correct participle in a word bank — students only need to conjugate "haber" and assemble.

4 Presente perfecto — Applied Practice GrammarVocabulary

▼
🎯 Daily Objective (Can-Do)
I can ask and answer questions about work experiences using the present perfect and current jobs vocabulary in context.
✅ Activities & Sequence (~45 min)
  1. Warm-Up — Error Correction (5 min): Display 4 present perfect sentences with common errors (missing "haber," wrong participle, wrong subject-verb agreement). Students identify and correct each one in their notebooks. Review together.
  2. Interpersonal Activity: "Encuesta de experiencias" (15 min): Students receive a "Find Someone Who…" grid (6–8 boxes). Each box has a prompt: "Ha trabajado como cajero/a," "Ha cuidado niños," "Ha ganado dinero por su cuenta," "Ha tenido una entrevista," etc. Students circulate, asking classmates in Spanish: "¿Has trabajado como cajero?" If yes, they write that person's name and one detail. Must talk to at least 5 different classmates.
  3. Authentic Text or Video Connection (12 min): Show a short authentic resource — a Spanish-language social media post, article excerpt, or video clip where someone discusses their work experience using the present perfect. Students answer 3 comprehension questions. This exposes them to authentic language and models how the present perfect is used naturally.
  4. Collaborative Writing — "Nuestras experiencias" (10 min): In groups of 3, students write a short paragraph (5–6 sentences) summarizing what they learned from the "Encuesta." Model: "En nuestra clase, tres personas han trabajado como niñeros. Nadie ha sido salvavidas. Dos personas han ganado dinero por su cuenta." Each group shares one finding with the class.
  5. Wrap-Up & Preview (3 min): Remind students that tomorrow is the weekly written assessment. They will need to use the present perfect and the jobs vocabulary. Quick review of key irregular participles.
🔍 Check for Understanding
Circulate during "Encuesta": Listen for correct formation of questions ("¿Has + participio?"). Note students who default to English or avoid using the structure. The collaborative paragraph is also a formative check — collect or photograph each group's paragraph to review accuracy.
🗣 Student Discourse Strategy
"Find Someone Who" with Accountability: This is a high-movement, high-engagement activity that 8th graders love. Key management tips: (1) Set a timer — 10 minutes max for circulating. (2) Require they ask the FULL question in Spanish (not just point at the box). (3) They must write the person's name AND one follow-up detail to prevent superficial check-offs. (4) Debrief as a whole class so there's a purpose to the data collected.
⚖ Differentiation
Heritage/Native Speakers ↑: Their "Encuesta" grid includes more complex prompts: "Ha emprendido un proyecto personal," "Ha solicitado empleo formalmente." During collaborative writing, they serve as the scribe and must add 2 sentences using vocabulary beyond the basic 5 jobs. For the authentic text, they answer comprehension questions AND write a personal connection response.
Additional Support ↓: The "Encuesta" grid includes the full question pre-written (students read it aloud rather than forming it from scratch). For collaborative writing, provide a paragraph frame with blanks: "En nuestra clase, ______ personas han ______. Nadie ha ______." The authentic text activity includes a glossary of unfamiliar words.

5 Weekly Assessment — Reflexión escrita Gradebook

▼
🎯 Daily Objective (Can-Do)
I can write a reflection about my work and volunteer experiences (real or imagined) using the present perfect tense and current jobs vocabulary accurately.
✅ Activities & Sequence (~45 min)
  1. Warm-Up — Vocab & Grammar Quick Review (7 min): Project a rapid-fire slide with 5 vocab images and 5 present perfect conjugation prompts. Students complete them on scratch paper. Do NOT collect — this is a last-minute review and confidence builder before the assessment.
  2. Assessment Introduction & Modeling (5 min): Display the writing prompt on the board and read it aloud. Show a brief model paragraph (3–4 sentences) to illustrate expectations. Clarify: they may write about REAL experiences or IMAGINED ones. Stress: use the present perfect at least 4 times, include at least 3 vocabulary words from the unit.
  3. Written Assessment — "Mi experiencia laboral" (25 min): Students write a paragraph of 8–10 sentences on Canvas (text entry) or on paper.

    Prompt: "Escribe una reflexión sobre tus experiencias con el trabajo o el voluntariado. ¿Has tenido un trabajo? ¿Has ayudado a alguien? ¿Qué has aprendido? Si no has trabajado, imagina: ¿qué trabajo te gustaría tener y qué habrías hecho en ese puesto? Usa el presente perfecto por lo menos 4 veces y por lo menos 3 palabras del vocabulario de la unidad."
  4. Peer Read & React (5 min): After submitting, students swap with a partner (or read a classmate's Canvas submission). They write one positive comment ("Me gusta que mencionaste…") and one question ("¿Has pensado en…?") — in Spanish. This adds a discourse element even to an assessment day.
  5. Week 1 Wrap-Up (3 min): Revisit Can-Do Statements. Students re-mark their progress. Preview Week 2: "Next week we'll explore career paths and what jobs DO."
📋 Gradebook Entry — Week 1 All-Task

"Mi experiencia laboral" — Written Reflection

Mode: Presentational Writing  |  Time: 25 min  |  Points: Rubric-based (use adjusted MCPS Intermediate Learner Presentational Rubric)

Prompt: Write 8–10 sentences reflecting on your work/volunteer experiences using the present perfect (minimum 4 uses) and unit vocabulary (minimum 3 words).

Task Completion: Addresses all parts of prompt; meets length requirement
Vocabulary: Uses 3+ unit vocabulary words accurately and in context
Grammar: Correct present perfect formation (haber + participle) in 4+ instances
Comprehensibility: Writing is understandable to a sympathetic reader; ideas are connected
🔍 Check for Understanding
The assessment IS the CFU. Review student submissions for: (1) Are students forming the present perfect correctly? (2) Are they using vocabulary in context or just listing words? (3) Common errors to address in Week 2's warm-ups. Flag students scoring below expectations for targeted re-teaching during Week 2.
🗣 Student Discourse Strategy
Peer Read & React: Even on assessment days, build in discourse. The 5-minute peer feedback at the end is low-stakes and adds an interpersonal reading+writing micro-task. Provide sentence starters for feedback: "Me gusta que mencionaste ______" and "Una pregunta que tengo es ______." This also gives early finishers something meaningful to do.
⚖ Differentiation
Heritage/Native Speakers ↑: Extended prompt — write 12+ sentences and include at least 2 irregular past participles. Add a concluding paragraph: "¿Qué has aprendido de tus experiencias que te ayudará en el futuro?" They should also demonstrate more sophisticated vocabulary beyond the basic 5 jobs (e.g., desempeñar, emprender, ocuparse de). Peer feedback must include a suggestion, not just a compliment.
Additional Support ↓: Reduced requirement: 5–6 sentences, 3 present perfect uses, 2 vocabulary words. Provide a writing scaffold on paper: sentence starters for each part of the prompt ("Yo he ______," "También he ______," "He aprendido que ______," "Un trabajo que me interesa es ______ porque ______"). Allow access to the vocabulary list and conjugation chart during writing.
