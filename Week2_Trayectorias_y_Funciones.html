Week 2: Las trayectorias profesionales y las funciones del trabajo

Focus: Explore career paths and fields of study, learn job function verbs, and connect careers to actions through discourse-heavy activities. Present perfect continues to spiral through warm-ups.

  • Vocabulary: Las trayectorias profesionales (abogado/a, científico/a, contador/a, empresario/a, ingeniero/a, programador/a, campo, carrera militar, escuela vocacional, especializarse, sacarle provecho, tomar cursos) + Las funciones del trabajo (atender, construir, cuidar, defender, investigar, salvar)
  • Grammar: Present perfect spiral (warm-ups) + introduction of function verbs in present subjunctive context (preview)
  • Culture: Programa Juventud y Empleo (Dominican Republic), career paths in Spanish-speaking countries, study requirements
  • Modes: Interpretive Interpersonal Presentational
  • Weekly Gradebook Entry: Career Exploration Digital Poster — matching careers to functions with explanations (Day 5)

6 Las trayectorias profesionales — Career Paths VocabularyCulture

▼
🎯 Daily Objective (Can-Do)
I can identify at least 6 career paths in Spanish and explain what field of study each career requires.
✅ Activities & Sequence (~45 min)
  1. Warm-Up — Presente perfecto spiral (5 min): "¿Qué has hecho este fin de semana?" Students write 2 sentences using the present perfect. Share with a partner. Quick whole-class share (2–3 volunteers). This keeps Week 1 grammar alive.
  2. Career Paths Vocabulary Introduction (12 min): Use Slides 10, 12, and 13. Present each career with its field of study: "Para ser abogado/a, estudio el campo de derecho." Students create a two-column T-chart in notebooks: Carrera | Campo de estudio. Include all 6 careers + escuela vocacional + carrera militar. Choral repetition of pronunciation for each.
  3. Video: Programa Juventud y Empleo (10 min): Play the video referenced on Slide 11 (or a substitute authentic clip about youth career programs in Latin America). Before viewing, give students 3 focus questions: ¿Qué carreras mencionan? ¿Para quién es el programa? ¿Qué ofrece? After viewing, students discuss answers at tables before whole-class debrief.
  4. "¿Qué quieres ser?" Partner Conversation (10 min): Students pair up. Each takes turns answering: "¿Qué quieres ser en el futuro?" and "Para esa carrera, ¿qué necesitas estudiar?" Sentence frames on the board: "Quiero ser ______ y necesito estudiar ______. También puedo tomar cursos de ______ para sacarle provecho." Partners must ask at least one follow-up question.
  5. Vocabulary Sorting Activity (8 min): In groups of 3, students receive cut-out cards (or digital drag-and-drop on Canvas) with career names, fields of study, and brief descriptions jumbled together. They sort and match them correctly. First group finished with all correct matches earns a small reward. Review answers as a class.
🔍 Check for Understanding
Exit Ticket — 3-2-1: On a slip of paper or Canvas: 3 career paths you remember in Spanish, 2 fields of study that go with them, and 1 career you'd like to learn more about. Quick scan tells you who absorbed the vocabulary and who needs reinforcement.
🗣 Student Discourse Strategy
Partner Conversation with Required Follow-Up: The key 8th-grader management move is requiring the follow-up question. Post 3 sample follow-up questions on the board: "¿Por qué te interesa eso?" / "¿Conoces a alguien que trabaje en ese campo?" / "¿Qué cursos tomarías?" This prevents the conversation from dying after one exchange. Circulate with a clipboard and tally who is on task.
⚖ Differentiation
Heritage/Native Speakers ↑: During partner conversation, they must explain WHY they are interested in a career using at least 3 sentences. They can also add careers not on the official list (médico, maestro, artista) and explain the corresponding field of study. For the video, they answer an additional inference question: "¿Por qué crees que este programa es necesario en ese país?"
Additional Support ↓: Provide the T-chart partially completed (3 of 8 rows filled as models). For the partner conversation, give a full script template they can customize with their own career choice. The sorting activity includes visual icons alongside text to support comprehension.

7 Las funciones del trabajo — What Jobs DO Vocabulary

▼
🎯 Daily Objective (Can-Do)
I can describe what people do in different jobs using 6 function verbs (atender, construir, cuidar, defender, investigar, salvar) and match functions to specific careers.
✅ Activities & Sequence (~45 min)
  1. Warm-Up — Career Path Recall (5 min): Quick-draw: teacher describes a career in Spanish without naming it ("Esta persona estudia derecho y defiende a sus clientes"). Students write the career on whiteboards and hold up. Do 4–5 rounds. This reviews yesterday's vocab and previews today's function verbs.
  2. Function Verbs Introduction with Slides 14–19 (15 min): Walk through each verb with the paired visuals from the unit presentation. For each verb, show it connected to TWO jobs (one current/youth job, one career): e.g., "Atender — el camarero atiende a los clientes / la contadora atiende a sus clientes." Students add to a vocabulary graphic organizer: Verbo | Significado | ¿Quién lo hace?
  3. "¿Qué hace?" Guessing Game (10 min): Teacher (or a student volunteer) acts out or describes a job function without naming the career. Class guesses: "¿Es alguien que cuida niños? ¡Es un niñero!" Then transition to partner version: Student A picks a card with a function verb, describes what the person does; Student B guesses the career. Play 3 rounds each.
  4. Matching Practice — Slides 21–22 (8 min): Use the practice slides from the unit presentation. Students work in pairs to match job postings to the correct career. Discuss answers as a class, emphasizing the function verbs used in each posting.
  5. Quick Write (7 min): "Escribe 4 oraciones conectando una función con una carrera." Model: "Un científico investiga nuevas medicinas." Students write independently, then compare with a partner. Did you use the same careers? Different ones?
🔍 Check for Understanding
Quick Write Review: Collect or photograph the 4-sentence quick writes. Check for: (1) correct verb conjugation, (2) logical career-function pairing, (3) variety of verbs used. Students who only use 1–2 verbs may need more exposure tomorrow.
🗣 Student Discourse Strategy
Guessing Game with Cards: Physical cards create structure and prevent off-task behavior. Each student gets exactly 3 cards, so they know exactly how many rounds they play. The competitive element (guessing correctly) motivates participation. For reluctant speakers, the card gives them a script — they describe what's on the card rather than generating language from scratch.
⚖ Differentiation
Heritage/Native Speakers ↑: During the guessing game, they must give 3-sentence descriptions (not just one clue). Their quick write should include 6 sentences and use at least one stem-changing verb correctly (atender → atiende, defender → defiende). Bonus: write a sentence explaining a function verb not on the list (enseñar, diseñar, programar).
Additional Support ↓: Provide the graphic organizer with the verb meanings pre-filled in English — students only need to add "¿Quién lo hace?" For the guessing game, their cards include the description pre-written (they read it aloud). Quick write sentence frame: "Un/a ______ [verbo] ______ en su trabajo."

8 Connecting Careers & Functions — Discourse Day VocabularyCulture

▼
🎯 Daily Objective (Can-Do)
I can discuss which careers interest me and why, explaining the functions involved, and I can compare career opportunities for youth in different countries.
✅ Activities & Sequence (~45 min)
  1. Warm-Up — "Descríbelo" (5 min): One student sits facing away from the board. The class sees a career name projected. They must describe the career using function verbs (no English, no cognate cheating). The seated student guesses. Do 3 rounds with different volunteers. High energy, great for 8th graders.
  2. Authentic Text: "Cómo buscar empleo" (15 min): Use the video from Slide 20 or the model activity "Al momento de buscar empleo." Pre-teach 3–4 key unfamiliar words. Students read/watch, then answer 4 comprehension questions individually. Partner check: compare answers and discuss any disagreements before whole-class review.
  3. Four Corners Debate (12 min): Post 4 career categories in the room's corners: Universidad, Escuela vocacional, Carrera militar, Trabajar inmediatamente. Read a scenario: "Marcos tiene 18 años. Le gusta construir cosas y trabajar con las manos. ¿Qué debería hacer?" Students move to the corner they think fits best. In their corner group, they discuss WHY for 2 minutes using sentence frames. One spokesperson shares with the class. Do 2–3 scenarios.
  4. Cultural Comparison — Pair Discussion (8 min): Display a brief infographic or data comparison: career training paths in the U.S. vs. a Spanish-speaking country (e.g., the "exigencia de estudios" concept). Partners discuss: "¿Qué es diferente? ¿Qué es similar? ¿Cuál prefieres y por qué?" Sentence frames posted.
  5. Wrap-Up & Preview (5 min): Quick review of all vocabulary covered this week so far (jobs + careers + functions). Preview tomorrow's "job fair" simulation. Students should think about which career they'd want to "represent."
🔍 Check for Understanding
Four Corners Observation: As students discuss in their corners, circulate and listen. Are they using function verbs? Career vocabulary? Note which students default to English or struggle to justify their choice. The authentic text comprehension questions also serve as a written check.
🗣 Student Discourse Strategy
Four Corners Debate: This is one of the most effective discourse strategies for 8th graders because it involves MOVEMENT. Key management: (1) Students must walk, not run. (2) Once in a corner, they have 2 minutes to prepare — set a visible timer. (3) Assign a spokesperson RANDOMLY (draw a name) so everyone prepares. (4) Limit to 2–3 scenarios to keep energy high without losing control. (5) Post the sentence frame: "Creo que Marcos debería ______ porque en ese campo, él puede ______."
⚖ Differentiation
Heritage/Native Speakers ↑: During Four Corners, they present counterarguments: "Entiendo que ustedes piensan ______, pero yo creo que ______ porque ______." For the authentic text, they write a 3-sentence personal reaction: "¿Qué consejo de este texto te parece más útil? ¿Por qué?" Cultural comparison: they draw on family experience to add authentic perspectives.
Additional Support ↓: For Four Corners, provide a "cheat sheet" with one argument per corner pre-written that students can reference. The authentic text includes a glossary sidebar. During the cultural comparison, provide a Venn diagram template with 2 similarities already filled in as models.

9 Mini Feria de Empleo — Simulated Job Fair VocabularyCulture

▼
🎯 Daily Objective (Can-Do)
I can present information about a career (functions, study requirements, and why it matters) and ask and answer questions about different careers in a simulated job fair setting.
✅ Activities & Sequence (~45 min)
  1. Warm-Up — Prep Time (7 min): Students were told yesterday to think about a career. Now they have 7 minutes to prepare a small "booth" — a single sheet of paper or a quick Google Slide with: the career name, the field of study, 2 functions of the job, and one reason it appeals to them. Everything in Spanish.
  2. Mini Feria de Empleo — Round 1 (12 min): Half the class stands at their "booths" (desks). The other half circulates. Visitors must ask at least 2 questions at each booth: "¿Qué hace un/a ______?" and "¿Qué necesitas estudiar?" Booth holders present their career and answer questions. Visitors take notes on a "Feria" recording sheet: career, function, and one interesting detail.
  3. Mini Feria de Empleo — Round 2 (12 min): Switch roles. New booth holders, new visitors. Same structure. By the end, every student has both presented AND visited multiple booths.
  4. Debrief & Share (8 min): Back in seats. Whole-class discussion: "¿Qué carrera te pareció más interesante de las que visitaste? ¿Por qué?" 4–5 students share. Teacher highlights strong vocabulary use heard during the fair.
  5. Preview Assessment (6 min): Introduce tomorrow's gradebook assignment: a Career Exploration Digital Poster. Explain expectations: pick one career, explain its functions, study requirements, and personal connection. Show a brief model. Students begin brainstorming their career choice.
🔍 Check for Understanding
Feria Recording Sheet: Collect the recording sheets at the end. These show you: (1) How many booths each student visited (engagement), (2) Whether they recorded accurate information (comprehension), (3) Quality of the "interesting detail" (depth of interaction). Students who visited fewer than 3 booths may need engagement support.
🗣 Student Discourse Strategy
Simulated Job Fair — Structured Rotation: This is PEAK discourse. Every student speaks in every round. Management keys: (1) Assign booth positions — don't let students choose or they'll cluster with friends. (2) Use a bell/timer to signal rotations every 3 minutes. (3) The recording sheet creates accountability — they must write something at each booth. (4) Post required questions on the board so visitors always have something to ask. (5) Walk the room with a positive feedback tally: "I heard great Spanish at Table 4!"
⚖ Differentiation
Heritage/Native Speakers ↑: Their booth presentation must include 4+ functions and a comparison to how this career works in their heritage country. As visitors, they ask higher-order follow-up questions: "¿Cuáles son los retos de esa carrera?" and "¿Qué competencias necesitas?" (previewing Week 4 vocabulary). They also serve as "language consultants" — classmates can ask them vocabulary questions during the fair.
Additional Support ↓: Provide a booth template with fill-in-the-blank structure: "Mi carrera es ______. Para ser ______, necesito estudiar ______. En este trabajo, una persona [verbo] ______. Me interesa porque ______." As visitors, their recording sheet has the questions pre-printed, and they circle/check off responses rather than writing from scratch.

10 Weekly Assessment — Career Exploration Poster Gradebook

▼
🎯 Daily Objective (Can-Do)
I can create a digital poster about a career that includes the career path, field of study, job functions, and a personal connection, using vocabulary from Weeks 1–2 accurately.
✅ Activities & Sequence (~45 min)
  1. Warm-Up — Vocabulary Review Blitz (5 min): Rapid-fire: teacher says a definition or function in Spanish, students write the matching vocabulary word. 8 items covering both weeks of vocabulary. Not collected — confidence builder before the assessment.
  2. Assessment Overview & Model (5 min): Display the rubric and a model poster (1 Google Slide or Canva template). Walk through each required section. Answer clarifying questions. Remind students: all content must be in Spanish. They may use their vocabulary notes but NOT Google Translate.
  3. Digital Poster Creation (28 min): Students create a 1-page digital poster (Google Slides or Canva on Chromebooks) about a career of their choice.

    Required sections:
    • La carrera: Name of the career and an image
    • El campo de estudio: What you need to study and where (universidad, escuela vocacional, etc.)
    • Las funciones: At least 3 things this professional does (using function verbs)
    • Conexión personal: Why this career interests you (3+ sentences using present perfect: "He aprendido que..." "Siempre he querido...")
    • Vocabulario bonus: Use at least 5 unit vocabulary words total, highlighted or underlined
  4. Peer Gallery & Feedback (5 min): Students share their poster link with one partner. Partner writes one "glow" (something strong) and one "grow" (a suggestion) in Spanish on a sticky note or Canvas comment.
  5. Submit & Week 2 Wrap-Up (2 min): Submit to Canvas. Quick Can-Do self-check. Preview Week 3: "Next week we dive into the hiring process and learn a new grammar structure — the subjunctive!"
📋 Gradebook Entry — Week 2 All-Task

"Explora una carrera" — Career Exploration Digital Poster

Mode: Presentational Writing  |  Time: 28 min  |  Points: Rubric-based (adjusted MCPS Intermediate Learner Presentational Rubric)

Requirements: 1-page digital poster with career name, field of study, 3+ job functions, personal connection (3+ sentences with present perfect), and 5+ vocabulary words.

Task Completion: All 4 required sections present; meets vocabulary minimum (5 words)
Vocabulary: Accurate use of career paths, function verbs, and job-related terms in context
Grammar: Correct use of present perfect in personal connection section; overall verb accuracy
Comprehensibility & Presentation: Clear, organized poster understandable to a sympathetic reader; visual supports enhance meaning
🔍 Check for Understanding
The poster IS the CFU. When grading, look for: (1) Are students choosing logical function verbs for their career? (2) Do they understand the difference between "campo" and "carrera"? (3) Is the present perfect used correctly in the personal connection section? (4) Common errors to address in Week 3 warm-ups. Students below expectations get a brief re-teaching session during Week 3 Day 1 warm-up.
🗣 Student Discourse Strategy
Peer Gallery with Structured Feedback: Even on assessment day, the 5-minute peer feedback window adds an interpersonal micro-task. Require the "glow" and "grow" to be in Spanish. Post sentence frames: "Me gusta que incluyeste ______" (glow) and "Podrías añadir más sobre ______" (grow). This keeps early finishers engaged and adds a reading comprehension layer as they interpret a classmate's poster.
⚖ Differentiation
Heritage/Native Speakers ↑: Extended requirements: 5+ functions (not just 3), personal connection must be 5+ sentences and include a comparison to how this career is pursued in their heritage country. Must use at least 2 vocabulary words from the "additional" list (emprender, desempeñar, sacarle provecho). Peer feedback must include a substantive question about the career.
Additional Support ↓: Provide a poster template with labeled sections and sentence starters already in place: "Mi carrera es ______. Para ser ______, necesito estudiar ______. Un/a ______ [verbo 1] ______. También [verbo 2] ______. Me interesa porque he ______." Reduce function verb requirement to 2. Allow vocabulary reference sheet during creation.
